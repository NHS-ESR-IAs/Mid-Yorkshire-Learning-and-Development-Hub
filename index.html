    I have completed some training and my compliance has not been updated, what can I do?
    • E-learning: Please ensure you have completed the e-learning through a learning certificate, and that you have clicked the house button to exit the course.
    • Face-to-face/MS Teams: Please allow 10 working days for class sessions to be updated, as these are processed manually.
    • If your competencies still have not been updated, please contact Organisational Development on midyorks.org.dev@nhs.net
    Is mandatory training that is completed in another organisation recognised by Mid Yorkshire Teaching NHS Trust?

    Some training may differ from trust to trust, but most should transfer when joining via the IAT process. If any training is incorrect or hasn’t transferred, please email midyorks.org.dev@nhs.net. The team can investigate and process any documentation, screenshots, or learning records manually.

    What do I do if my certification subscription status is "expired"?

    Once your certification has expired, you’ll need to renew the competency. If you’re struggling to access it or have queries, email midyorks.nlms@nhs.net Be sure to complete training shortly after accessing the course—if a competency expires mid-progress, your progress may reset.

    What do I do if I believe the 'My Compliance' portlet is showing the incorrect requirement for my role, including requirements that are missing.

    Email midyorks.org.dev@nhs.net with the details of the incorrect or missing requirement so they can review them on your behalf.

    Greyed out or missing 'Play' button - How to access the course in a different way
    1. On your ESR Homepage, click ‘My Compliance’ under the Learning and Development Portlet. Select the course and click the magnifying glass.
    2. If not subscribed, click ‘Details’, then ‘Subscribe’, and enrol on the course.
    3. On the next page, click ‘Enrol’ onto the course modules.
    4. If the green play button is still unavailable, use the ‘Select an action’ drop-down and click ‘Play’.

    Aspiring Medical Leader Programme

    Appraisal Skills for Managers/Appraisers (Non Medical)

    Appraisal Skills for Managers/Appraisers (Non Medical)

    Effective performance appraisal and staff development contribute directly to improved patient outcomes. That is why regulators such as the Care Quality Commission and the Department of Health and Social Care emphasise the importance of appraisal and development reviews.

    Every member of staff should have an annual appraisal, including a six-monthly review (as a minimum), to ensure regular dialogue throughout the year. This gives staff a chance to receive feedback and reflect on progress against agreed objectives.

    Appraisals are also a key opportunity to:

    • Reflect on Trust Values and Behaviours
    • Develop a Personal Development Plan (PDP)
    • Support professional requirements such as NMC revalidation

    All new staff should receive an appraisal within 3 months of starting their role. For details, visit the Trust Policies intranet page.

    Appraisals must be recorded via ESR. A downloadable handout is available to help with this process.

    📄 Accessing Appraisal Documentation

    1. Go to Departments on the Intranet.
    2. Click H and select Human Resources.
    3. Choose the box at the top of the page labelled "During Employment".
    4. Scroll past "Developing Your Team" to find the Appraisals drop-down menu containing all relevant documentation.

    The Embedding Values & Behaviours programme was introduced in late 2018. It provides leadership development for Band 7 and above, empowering the organisation through a compassionate lens.

    ILM5 in Coaching & Mentoring

    ILM5 in Coaching & Mentoring

    This is a highly participative programme accredited by the ILM. It offers a practical grounding in the distinctions between coaching and mentoring, and explores how coaching can support individual and organisational growth.

    You’ll gain the knowledge and skills to become a confident, well-practiced coach, capable of contributing to a robust coaching service within Mid Yorkshire Teaching NHS Trust.

    🌱 What We Ask of Our Coaches

    • Coach a minimum of two individuals per year
    • Offer up to six sessions per person (face-to-face or virtual), typically 90 minutes each
    • Attend at least two supervision sessions every 12 months
    • Maintain standards through CPD
    • Follow agreed coaching systems, including session records and CPD evidence

    📚 Programme Structure

    • Attendance of all 8 workshops blending theory with practice
    • Tutorial support and coaching supervision throughout
    • Completion of 3 written assignments:
      • Assignment 1: Theory paper on the role of coaching and mentoring (min. 5000 words)
      • Assignment 2: Coaching portfolio with 18 hours of recorded practice and reflections
      • Assignment 3: Reflective journal demonstrating your competence
    • Completion of a recorded coaching session outside the taught programme

    This course is ideal for staff who wish to actively coach within the Trust and meet the participation criteria listed above. A manager’s statement of support must be completed as part of your application.

    For further information, please email midyorks.org.dev@nhs.net.
